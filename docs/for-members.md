# You've been given members access

A site you visit has a members-only area, and the person who runs it added you to
it. Here's how to get in.

## Signing in

1. Go to any members page on the site — anything under `yoursite.com/members/` — or
   to the site's members login page (`yoursite.com/members-login.html`). If you try a
   members page while signed out, you'll be sent to the login page automatically, and
   after signing in you'll land right back where you were headed.
2. Click **Continue with Google**.
3. Use the **exact email address the owner added for you**. If they added your
   personal address and you sign in with your work one, it won't match.

You need a Google account on that email. Gmail addresses have one automatically, and
many work addresses do too. If your email has no Google account, you can create one
for it for free at [accounts.google.com](https://accounts.google.com/signup) (choose
"Use your existing email"), or ask the owner to add an address of yours that does.

## How long access lasts

The owner sets a duration when they add you: anywhere from 1 to 360 days, or no
expiry at all. While it lasts, members pages just work in that browser. When it runs
out, you'll be bounced to the login page again — sign in the same way and you're back
in. If that stops working, your access may have lapsed and the owner can renew it in
a few seconds.

## "You're not on the list (yet)"

This message means the Google account you signed in with isn't on the site's member
list. It usually comes down to one of two things:

- You used a different email than the one the owner added. Try the other one.
- The owner hasn't added you yet (or your access expired). Ask them to add your email
  under **People** in their site's admin.

Nothing is broken, and there's nothing to fix on your end beyond using the right
address.

## What members access covers

Everything under the site's `/members/` section: pages, and also files like PDFs
linked from those pages. It does not let you edit anything — if you were also invited
to edit the site, that's a separate sign-in, covered in
[the editor guide](for-editors.md).
